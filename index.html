📝 Règles grammaticales

Le Prétérit (Past Simple)

✅ Forme affirmative

Sujet + V-ed (réguliers) / forme irrégulière + complément

She worked yesterday.  |  He went to school.  |  They had a meeting.

❌ Forme négative

Sujet + did not (didn't) + base verbale + complément

She didn't work.  |  He didn't go to school.

⚠️ On utilise la base verbale après didn't — jamais le prétérit !

❓ Forme interrogative

Did + sujet + base verbale + complément ?

Did she work?  |  Did he go to school?

💡 Did est invariable — même forme pour tous les sujets.

Le Present Perfect (Have + Past Participle)

✅ Forme affirmative

Sujet + have / has + participe passé + complément

She has eaten.  |  They have gone to Paris.

❌ Forme négative

Sujet + have not (haven't) / has not (hasn't) + participe passé

She hasn't eaten.  |  They haven't gone.

❓ Forme interrogative

Have / Has + sujet + participe passé + complément ?

Has she eaten?  |  Have they gone?

🆚 Prétérit vs Present Perfect

Past Simple

Action terminée à un moment précis du passé

She left yesterday. / In 2019.

Present Perfect

Lien avec le présent ou expérience de vie

She has left. / I have never been there.
